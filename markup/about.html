About Yours Truly

The Basics

I'm a dude that writes software. That's what I do to keep the lights on (and sometimes just for fun). I also do things that don't require computer machines. I've been strengh-training since 2017, I've been gaming since the early 80's and golfing since the mid 90's. I also like reading the interwebz and occasionally the written page.

That's my profession and those are my hobbies. The things that really matter are the luckiest girl in the world, my wife, and our two adopted, half-crazy, four-thirds wonderful dogs. Did I mention I'm bad at math? (I'm not really bad at math, potential employers/clients). The Mrs. and the mutts are the reason I get up in the morning and stare at a computer screen half of my waking hours. Well, them and cheeseburgers. And pizza. And video games. And shiny new golf stuff. But, moooooostly the wife and dogs...

How Did I Become a Software Engineer?

I'm so glad you asked! As I mentioned up there ^, I'm a gamer. I have been since I was about five-years old with the Atari 2400. While I don't get to game as much as I used to, I still find time to plug in and frag some bad guys. As with most life-long gamers, I ended up building my own gaming rigs in the late 90's and early 00's. It was rig-building that lead to my Eureka! moment: I could do computer stuff for money. And money means...more computer stuff!

So, I went back to college after a not-at-all successful start to my collegiate career which included a suspension for academic reasons. Those reasons being too many F's, not enough A's. C'mon, I started out in nursing and then thought pursuing an accounting degree was a good idea. Of course I stopped going to class. God bless nurses and accountants (maybe not accountants) -- I'm not cut out for either profession as I hate both needles and spreadsheets equally.

After a few years and enough credits to constitute an Associates Degree in Computer Information Systems (Computer Stuff), I landed my first job as a software engineer in 2006. The rest, as they say, is history.

PS - I did finish my Bachelors in Computer Stuff while doing computer stuff for money. It's sorta like recursion! ...but not really. 😏
