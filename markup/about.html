About Yours Truly

The Basics

I'm a dude that writes software. That's what I do to keep the lights on, dogs fed and cheesburgers in my belly (and sometimes just for fun). I also do things that don't require infernal computer machines. I've been strengh-training since 2017, I've been gaming since the early 80's and golfing since the mid 90's. I also like reading the interwebz and occasionally the written page.

That's my profession and those are my hobbies. The things that really matter are luckiest girl in the world, my wife, and our two adopted, half-crazy, two-thirds perfect dogs. Did I mention I'm bad at math? That's why I let the computer machines do it for me! (I'm not really bad at math, potential employers/clients). The Mrs. and the mutts are the reason I get up in the morning and stare at a computer screen half of my waking hours. Well, them and the aforementioned cheeseburgers. And pizza. And video games. And shiny new golf stuff. But, yah, mostly the wife and dogs...

How Did I Became an Engineer?

I'm so glad you asked! As I mentioned up there ^, I'm a video-gamer. I have been since I was about five-years old with the Atari 2400. While I don't get to game as much as I used to, I still find time to plug in and frag some bad guys. As with most life-long gamers, I ended up building my own gaming rigs in the late 90's and early 00's. It was that step that lead me to the conclusion that, 'Ya know, I could do computer stuff for money. And money means...more computer stuff.'

So, I went back to college after a not-at-all successful start to my collegiate career which included a suspension for academic reasons. Those reasons basically amounted to too many F's, not enough A's. C'mon, I started out in nursing and then thought pursuing an accounting degree was a good idea. Of course I stopped going to class. God bless nurses and accountants (maybe not accountants), but I'm not cut out for either. So, I got my degree in Computer Information Systems, i.e., "Computer Stuff", from Youngstown State University.

After a few years and enough credits to constitute an Associates Degree in 2006, a friend of mine and his brother gave me my first job as a software engineer at the company he co-founded. Befriending really smart people ftw! The rest, as they say, is history.

PS - I did finish my Bachelors Degree while doing computer stuff for money. 😏
