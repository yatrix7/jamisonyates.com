How I Got Into Software Engineering

I'm a video-gamer. I have been since I was about five-years old. While I don't get to game as much as I used to, I still find time to plug in and frag some bad guys.

I started out as a console game with the Atari 2400, ColecoVision then the NES and eventually got into PC gaming. Eventually, I bought my first PC, a kick-ass Gateway with a 266ghz Pentium II processor running Windows 95. I was officially part of the PC Master Race, looking down on the console gaming peasants I once called kin. Ah, but alas, my elevated station among the gaming elite was short-lived. I fell from grace in the early 2000's and rejoined the peasants on consoles. I hope that history and the gaming gods judge me kindly.

Anyway, PC-gaming inevitably lead to building my own gaming rigs (it always does). At some point in building PC's for myself and for others it hit me: I should do computer stuff for money!!! So, I went to school for "computer stuff", i.e., a Computer Information Systems degree. The rest, as they say, is history. Don't get me wrong, it wasn't easy or perfect. There were challenges, a lot of self-doubt and failure, but also a lot of growing and a lot more success than the aforementioned failure.

The moral of the story is this: if you're looking for a career that you'll enjoy and maybe find some fulfillment in, oftentimes the best place to look is your passions. There's likely something there that you can settle into, enjoy and make money to put back into your passions.
